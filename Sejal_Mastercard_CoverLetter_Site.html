Cover Letter

In my own words.

Dear Hiring Manager,

"The best process is not the fastest one — it is the one everyone actually follows."

Mastercard's mission — to power economies and empower people — is not a tagline; it is a blueprint for what technology-enabled infrastructure can achieve at scale. The chance to contribute to that mission from within, specifically by helping a 150+ person organisation operate more efficiently and communicate more clearly, is exactly the kind of role I have been building towards.

In my role as a Buy-Side Research and Investment Analyst at TresVista, I managed 120+ concurrent analytical mandates for institutional clients across private equity, hedge funds, and asset managers. Behind those mandates was a system: initiative trackers, structured workflows, status dashboards, briefing documents, and meeting actions — all maintained to the standard that senior clients expected, on 2-day turnaround timelines. That discipline — accurate capture, clear communication, consistent follow-through — is the core of what this role demands. I have lived it under pressure.

As Co-Founder of SAVIC Jewellery, I built the operational backbone of a growing business from scratch. I designed MIS dashboards, owned budget versus actuals reporting, coordinated a 10+ vendor network, and implemented process improvements that reduced invoice processing time by 7 days, improved operating margins by 20%, and cut receivables by 35%. These were not inherited systems. They were designed, tested, and refined — exactly the kind of incremental, data-driven improvement Mastercard's Process Improvement mandate calls for.

I am proficient in Microsoft Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, SharePoint, and Power BI. I hold an MSc Finance from Trinity College Dublin and a First Class BBA from Christ University. I am based full-time in Dublin and available to contribute from day one.

What motivates me most about this role is its dual nature — structured enough to demand precision, open enough to reward initiative. I am organised, detail-oriented, and energised by the challenge of making large teams work better. I would be proud to help Mastercard's operations team do exactly that.

Thank you sincerely for your time and consideration. I would welcome the opportunity to speak further.

Yours sincerely,
Sejal Manish Nagori
